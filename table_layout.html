Absolute vs. Relative Links

An absolute link referrs to links that provide complete location information of your website. The typical format for these links start with "https://" and will direct to the domain and extension. Relative links only contain the location following your domain and usually start with "../asset/img..." or "//" at the beginning. While both of these are types of links, absolute links allow you to link to somewhere or something outside of your direct domain.

The risk with this is that "hot linking," or using absolute links, can alter the route in which you are sending someone who clicks on it or when using images from another domain especially when using content without permission. If you hotlink an image on your website without the permission from the owner and they change the file to something with the same name, this could result in images or videos being changed. The good things about relative links on your own server or domain helps reduce loading times, saving you time, and simplifying your code.
